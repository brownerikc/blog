I once had a conversation with a coworker that touched on the idea of lessons learned throughout my career. As I thought about how to convey my ideas, my mind went to the idea of company value statements. The list of statements that a company says their employees should embody.

I'm generally not a fan of these. Not because they're necessarily bad or vapid (a lot of these statements are pretty agreeable, really). It's more that I think they approach the challenge of making employees care about their work from a top-down perspective that rarely sticks.

Put another way, when I was sorting through my ideas in this conversation, I realized that a company telling me they value adaptability means far less than me elaborating on why I feel adaptability is important. These values just feel more meaningful when they originate at the individual level.

Since then, I've had it in the back of my mind that it might be interesting to create a list of my own personal values. In fact, maybe more than interesting: it might help me focus and clarify why I care about my work and help me better act accordingly.

{/* truncate */}

After a bit of thought, I came up with a list of five value statements that I feel like I could reasonably elaborate on. Consider this an initial draft list. I might identify more later or tweak these.

- Have a north star
- Be adaptable
- Show the sawdust
- Embrace mistakes
- Make yourself known

I'll write a post for each of these in the future.
